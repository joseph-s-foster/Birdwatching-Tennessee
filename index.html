Birdwatching Tennessee

Southeastern Bluebird

Lifespan: 10 years

Wingspan: 9.8"

Fun Fact: Eastern BlueBirds aren't actually blue, the blue coloration is a trick of the light.

Nashville Warbler

Lifespan: 8 years

Wingspan: 7.5"

Fun Fact: The Nashville Warbler has never bred in Tennessee, the name comes from when when Alexander Wilson first saw the bird, while the bird was migrating.

Dark-Eyed Junco

Lifespan: 11 years

Wingspan: 9.25"

Fun Fact: The oldest known Dark-eyed Junco in the wild was 11 years 4 months.

Red Tailed Hawk

Lifespan: 20 years

Wingspan: 49"

Fun Fact: Filmmakers frequently use the cry of Red-tailed hawk to represent any hawk or eagle.

Downy Woodpecker

Lifespan: 5 years

Wingspan: 12"

Fun fact: The oldest Downy Woodpecker was 11 years 11 months

Eastern Screech Owl

Lifespan: 10 years

Wingspan: 20"

Fun fact: Eastern Screech Owls are usually monogamous and pairs remain together for life.

Northern Cardinal

Lifespan: 3-15 years

Wingspan: 12"

Fun Fact: When the female sings from the nest it's to inform the male whether to bring food to the nest.

House Finch

Lifespan: 11 years

Wingspan: 9.5"

Fun fact: The orange-red color of a male comes from the food they consume while molting.

Red-Bellied Woodpecker

Lifespan: 12 years

Wingspan: 16"

Fun fact: The male may tap loudly to attract a mate.

Blue Jay

Lifespan: 7 years

Wingspan: 16"

Fun fact: If a Blue Jay is crushed, the blue disappears as the structure is destroyed.

Ring Necked Dove

Lifespan: 15 years

Wingspan: 14"

Fun fact: Ring Necked Dove reach their adult size in around six weeks.

American Robin

Lifespan: 2 years

Wingspan: 17"

Fun fact: The oldest wild Robin in record was almost 14 years old.

Eastern Towhee

Lifespan: 12 years

Wingspan: 10.5"

Fun fact: Eastern Towhee have red eyes across the majority of their range, but in Florida and extreme southern Georgia, they have white eyes.

Mourning Dove

Lifespan: 4 years

Wingspan: 18"

Fun fact: Mourning Dove have been found to nest every month of the year.

Carolina Wren

Lifespan: 6 years

Wingspan: 7.5"

Fun fact: Females help the males with territorial defense by singing with their mates.
